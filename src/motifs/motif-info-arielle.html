By: Arielle Bishop
July 12, 2019

The boteh (meaning ‘bush) is the shape of a teardrop with a curve at the end, making up the pattern known today as paisley. It originated in Persian culture and then spread to India where it is referred to as the buta (meaning ‘flower’). The motif originated as a depiction of the cypress tree, symbolizing eternity and life, and is usually seen in conjunction with other nature-based symbols.
