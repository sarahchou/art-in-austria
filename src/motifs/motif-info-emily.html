Plaid

By: Emily Pillay
July 12, 2019

Plaid (Pattern): Plaid​, also known as “tartan” consists of horizontal and vertical lines of multiple colorsthat intersect to form squares of various colors. Plaid has its roots in Scottish cultureand was often worn in distinct color patterns to represent different Scottish clans. Theterms “plaid” and “tartan” became synonymous when the pattern was introduced in bothEuropean and American culture, initially through Scottish immigrants and later throughfashion trends. Today, the plaid pattern can be spotted at nearly any large clothing storeand it continues to be a popular style.

Origins: The oldest dating record of a plaid textile is said to be dated back to 8th century BC,belonging to a man of caucasian descent who was found buried in western China.Although that is the oldest dating record, the cultural origins of plaid are more oftenassociated with Scotland. The Scottish wore this textile in clothing with initially little colorvariety since resources were scarce. As time passed and resources became moreavailable through means of trade, different Scottish clans began to dress in specificcolor schemes of plaid. Plaid became an integral part of Scottish culture and laterproved to be a symbol for national pride.

Politics: During Scotland’s time of rebellion against the English monarchy, plaid become thepattern of the Scottish military uniform. At the time, a political group in Scotland was theJacobites, who were looking to have the House of Stuart rule over England, Scotland,and Ireland. The Jacobites faced the British in the Battle of Culloden, which took placein the Scottish Highlands. The Jacobites lost to the British, who then held the throne asthe House of Hanover. As the Hanovers were now ruling over England, Scotland andIreland, they wanted to put an end to the Jacobites and keep their place on the throne.In order to do this they had to gain control over the Scottish clan scene, one way ofwhich was by outlawing the classic plaid pattern that they all wore. As a result, the plaidpattern was outlawed for almost a century. After the ban had been lifted, plaid becamesymbolic of the unity and pride of Scotland. It also became a popular textile in Britishroyal attire, creating a gesture of peace between them and Scotland.

Fashion: Scottish immigrants brought the pattern of plaid with them and it became a populartextile for many fashion brands. In America, it became a popular pattern thatlumberjacks would sport. In more modern times, plaid became an integral part of thepunk fashion scene, where its embodiment of rebellion can still be seen today.

Color Development: The plaid color pallet has developed over time, mainly in relation to the resources thatwere available to those creating the textile. Before chemical dyes were around,vegetable dyes were used to produce plaid patterns that over time, often had light orfaded looking colors as a result of the dyes reacting to sunlight. Once chemical dyeswere introduced, much darker colors became a commonality in the plaid textiles. Themost common dark colors of blue, black, and green were used as this time and referredto as “modern” colors. Currently, plaid is mostly being produced for consumerism. As aresult, there is a wide color pallet that encompasses both “modern” colors and moreancient colors. These pallets have been referred to as “muted” colors, but someattempts to mimic vegetable dyes have left plaid patterns not very distinguishable as thecolors are of a similar hue. Overall, many of the traditional color pallet variations are stillseen in plaid today, but the evolution of then has mostly been driven by the resourcesavailable.

Popular Patterns

Black Watch: Variations of the Black Watch pattern were used to identify the Black Watch clan. TheBlack Watch pattern came in many variations, some of the most popular being Ancient,Modern, Weathered, Hunting, and Dress. The variations all featured a color pattern ofvarious combinations of navy, black, and green that ranged from bright to muted. Theclan itself was formed after the Jacobite Rebellion of 1715, with a goal of watching overthe Scottish Highlands. Six companies total from the Clans of Campbell, Fraser, Lovat,Munro, and Grant came together to form the Black Watch clan in 1725.

Stewart: Many different variations of the Stewart pattern were used to dress members of theStewart clan. The Stewart pattern came in many variations, some of the most popularbeing Ancient, Modern, Weathered, Hunting, and Dress. The variations featured a widerange of color pallets. From a pairing of white, green, and red to a pairing of red, green,yellow, white, blue, and black, many different patterns existed for the Stewart clan. TheStewart clan were the royal family of scotland up until the death of Queen Anne in 1714.As a result, they owned many elaborate palaces and castles in Scotland. At the timewhen the clan was in power, it also served as an army. One of the most notablepatterns of plaid from this clan is the Stewart Royal tartan which consists of a colorpallet of mainly scarlet red with accents of black, blue, green, yellow, and white.
