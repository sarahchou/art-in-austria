Mascaron

By: Jenny Testa
July 12, 2019

A mascaron is a sculptural representation of a head that is used as ornamental decoration inarchitecture, often above windows or doorways. The term “mascaron” comes from Latin“mascus”, meaning “head” or “face”.

As an architectural motif, mascarons can be traced back to ancient Egypt and thepre-Colombian era. Mascarons in Italy date back to pre-Roman times, and have been present inEuropean architecture ever since. Most mascarons exist as anonymous artwork, while few havebeen claimed to specific artists. The most recent movement to heavily feature mascarons wasthe Beaux Arts movement.

The mascaron motif originates from the mask, an artificial hollow face intended to conceal orgive an alternate identity to the wearer. Masks have been used throughout history for protection,disguise, performance, entertainment, and use in ritual or ceremony. Similarly, mascarons existin multiple forms in order to serve many varying uses or purposes.

Mascaron Forms and Meanings

Women: Women are commonly used as motifs for mascarons around the world. European femalemascarons are presented as beautiful and mysterious, while Russian female mascaronssymbolize purity. Many are thought to represent Greek goddess Hera, symbolizing protection ofthe family, hearth, and home.

Grotesque: Grotesque mascarons are composed of a combination of human, animal, and plant features,often to take the shape of an expressionistic monster. In the sixth and seventh centuriesgrotesque mascarons were built with the intention to ward off evil, but they eventually evolvedinto a more caricatured design that served a solely decorative purpose. They are most commonin Gothic architecture.

Green Man: The Green Man motif is a human, generally male, form surrounded by or composed of leavesand greenery. It symbolizes the rebirth of nature, and is commonly found as a decorativeornament.

Medusa Head: In Greek mythology, Medusa was a snake-haired monster who into stone any being that lookedinto her eyes. Therefore, traditionally the Medusa head motif was used to ward off evil. Archaeicart represents the Medusa head as ugly and monstrous, yet later Greek ideations displayed heras stern and beautiful. By the Modern and Renaissance eras, the Medusa head was seen asmainly decorative.

Skulls: The Skull or Death’s head is used to represent decay and death, and is generally found onmonuments and tombs. It is generally represented by a front view or placed over two crossedbones.

Animals: Animal mascarons commonly take the form of lions, tigers, panthers, goats, rams and horses.Lions have been the most popular form of animal mascaron since the Crusades of the 12thcentury, symbolizing strength, protection, and majesty. The tiger and panther were used inantique works, similarly symbolizing strength. Less adapted are oxen, pigs, dogs, foxes, stags,or hares, which are typically used symbolically in connection to hunting.

Other common mascaron forms involve fauns and satyrs, antique Gods, and angels.
